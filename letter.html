Exotic dance moves.

Yes, your right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances.To answer your question,

my favourite dances are as follows,with definitions:


Polynesian chicken dance

    A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC,a whole village would dance around

    in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".

Icelandic brownian shuffle

    Before the Icelanders developes fire as a means of getting warm,they used to practise this dance, which

    involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor,and shuffling thei bodies around in imperceptibly

    tiny,very rapid movements.One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired

    modern styles such as Twerking.

Arctic Robot Dance

    An intresting example of historic misinformation,English explorers in the 1960s believed to have

    discoverd a new dance style characterised by "robotic",stilted movements,being practised by inhabitants

    of Northern Alska and Canada.Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this

    because they were really cold.
